How It Works

This tool calculates your Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) based on the Semester Grade Point Averages (SGPA) you provide. The calculation follows the official method prescribed by MAKAUT (formerly WBUT).

The Formula

CGPA = (Sum of all Semester SGPA's) / (Total Number of Semesters)

For instance, if you have completed three semesters with SGPA scores of 8.5, 9.0, and 8.2, your CGPA would be calculated as:

(8.5 + 9.0 + 8.2) / 3 = 25.7 / 3 = 8.57

Your CGPA is 8.57.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What is the difference between SGPA and CGPA?

SGPA (Semester Grade Point Average) measures your performance in a single semester. CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is the average of all your SGPA scores, reflecting your overall academic performance across all semesters.

2. What is the 10-point grading scale in MAKAUT?

MAKAUT uses a 10-point grading scale where each letter grade (O, E, A, B, C, D, F) corresponds to a grade point. An 'O' (Outstanding) is 10 points, while an 'F' (Fail) is 0 points.

3. How many semesters can I add?

This tool allows you to add up to 8 semesters, covering a standard 4-year B.Tech program. The calculation adjusts based on the number of fields you fill in.

4. Why is my CGPA important?

Your CGPA is a key indicator of your academic standing. It is often used by companies for placements, by universities for higher studies admissions, and for scholarship eligibility.
